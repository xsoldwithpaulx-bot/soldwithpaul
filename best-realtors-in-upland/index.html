Frequently Asked Questions — Choosing a Realtor in Upland

What is the median home price in Upland, CA?
According to March 2026 listing data from Realtor.com, the median listing price in Upland is approximately $863,963 — essentially flat month-over-month (-2.2%). Upland's price-per-square-foot sits near $2,049, placing it among the highest-priced markets in San Bernardino County and in line with neighboring Rancho Cucamonga. The city spans three ZIP codes with significant pricing variation. The 91784 corridor (north Upland, including San Antonio Heights and the foothills above Mountain Avenue) commands the city's highest pricing, with custom-built homes and larger-lot single-family inventory routinely trading between $1.1 million and $2 million. The 91786 (south Upland, including Cabrillo Park and the Colonies area) represents the mid-tier segment between $700,000 and $900,000. The 91785 ZIP covers PO Box-only addresses and rarely has direct sales. Sellers should benchmark against specific ZIP and neighborhood rather than citywide averages.
How long does it take to sell a house in Upland?
The median days on market in Upland is currently 39 days based on March 2026 listing data — essentially unchanged from the prior month, indicating a stable seller's market. Properly priced Upland homes with professional photography and pre-listing preparation routinely close in under 30 days, particularly in the 91784 foothill corridor where buyer demand from Claremont, La Verne, and San Dimas move-down buyers remains consistent. Homes priced above comparable sales or lacking visual presentation tend to sit 60–90 days. The agents in this ranking typically list their Upland properties 10–15 days below the citywide median through disciplined pricing and faster response to offers. New listing volume in March 2026 expanded 35% from February, but absorption kept pace.
Is Upland a buyer's or seller's market right now?
Upland is a moderate seller's market as of early 2026. Active inventory sits at just 95 listings — among the tightest absolute counts in San Bernardino County — with 39-day median DOM and only 2 recent price reductions. These signals indicate that buyers have limited choice, particularly in the 91784 foothill corridor where inventory consistently runs below 40 active listings. However, listing prices are essentially flat month-over-month (-2.2%), which suggests pricing discipline rather than unchecked froth. The dynamic favors sellers who price realistically — the market absorbs well-priced listings quickly, but buyers have become more selective about which properties justify their asking prices. Over-priced or under-prepared listings in Upland still sit 60+ days despite tight overall inventory.
What are the best neighborhoods in Upland to sell a home?
Upland is organized around three ZIP codes with distinct seller dynamics. The 91784 ZIP (north Upland, including San Antonio Heights and the Mountain Avenue foothills) commands the city's highest pricing thanks to custom-built homes, larger lots, and the San Antonio Heights elevated views — properties routinely trade between $1.1 million and $2 million. The 91786 ZIP (south and central Upland, including Cabrillo Park, the Colonies, and downtown Upland) represents the mid-tier segment between $700,000 and $900,000 with consistent transaction volume — this is the deepest inventory segment. The 91785 ZIP covers PO Box-only addresses and rarely has direct sales. Pricing decisions should be made at the neighborhood level — the 91784 foothill segment is a fundamentally different market than 91786 mid-Upland and benefits from a different buyer pool. Upland sellers with 91784 properties often benefit from agents anchored in Claremont, La Verne, or Glendora who bring buyer pipeline from the east LA County luxury segment.
How do I choose the right real estate agent in Upland?
Match the agent's Upland-specific transaction history to your property's neighborhood and price segment. An agent with 20+ Upland sales in the last three years is categorically more useful than an agent with 200 career sales but only 5 in Upland — local market knowledge compounds at the ZIP and neighborhood level here. If your property is in the 91784 foothill corridor, ask specifically for 91784 transaction counts — foothill estate dynamics differ categorically from 91786 mid-Upland resale dynamics. Ask every agent you interview for three specific data points: their median days on market in your specific Upland neighborhood (not citywide), their list-to-sold ratio over the last 24 months, and the names of two recent Upland sellers in your price segment you can contact as references. With Upland's median at $864,000 and foothill properties trading above $1.1 million, commission structures matter in absolute-dollar terms — a 1% commission reduction on a median-priced Upland home represents $8,640 in seller proceeds; on a $1.5M foothill estate, it represents $15,000.
