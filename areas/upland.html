Living & Selling in Upland

Upland is the Inland Empire's best-performing seller's market right now — and the data backs it up. A 100.6% sale-to-list ratio (highest in Paul's IE group), 4.5% year-over-year appreciation (best in the region), and a DOM that's actually decreasing while every surrounding city gets slower. In a real estate landscape where "seller's market" has become aspirational for most IE cities, Upland is delivering it in the numbers.

North Upland is the luxury headliner: foothill properties with San Gabriel Mountain views, custom estates in the $900K-$2.5M range, and the Upland Hills Country Club gated community — exclusive homes that sold 21% over list in recent transactions. The Colonies, built on former citrus groves, offers mid-century character homes with large lots and renovation potential in the $650K-$1.1M range. Downtown Upland along A Street has become the city's lifestyle anchor — walkable dining, Saturday farmers markets, and a community character that draws buyers who value neighborhood identity over new construction. Sycamore Hills serves the family move-up tier with newer homes at $700K-$1M.

The school district is a genuine selling point: Upland Elementary and Floyd M. Stork both score 9/10, Upland High School rates 8/10, and the overall Upland USD is among the strongest in San Bernardino County. Buyers here are moving up from Ontario and Rancho Cucamonga, seeking foothill living, or relocating from the San Gabriel Valley where equivalent school quality costs significantly more. The pitch writes itself: San Gabriel Valley quality at Inland Empire prices.
