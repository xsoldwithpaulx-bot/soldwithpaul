Living & Selling in Claremont

Claremont — the "City of Trees and Ph.D.s" — is the most culturally distinctive real estate market in the Inland Empire. The Claremont Colleges consortium (Pomona, Claremont McKenna, Harvey Mudd, Scripps, Pitzer, and two graduate schools) creates a buyer pool that values education, walkability, and community character over square footage and new construction. This isn't a volume market — it's a prestige market, and sellers who understand that distinction price and present their homes accordingly.

North Claremont near the colleges commands $1.2M to $3M+ for tree-lined estates with character that no new build can replicate. Padua Hills brings historic Mediterranean architecture and the iconic amphitheater district. Stone Canyon's gated custom estates represent Claremont's ceiling at $2.3M median. The Village and College area — walkable to bookstores, the Saturday farmers market, and Claremont Boulevard dining — serves as the city's lifestyle anchor, drawing buyers who could afford more house elsewhere but choose Claremont for what surrounds it.

The school district reinforces property values at every level: Claremont High School scores 9/10, Condit Elementary hits 9/10, and the private school options connected to the colleges add another tier. Buyer demographics skew toward academics and professionals tied to the colleges, move-up buyers from the San Gabriel Valley seeking prestige, and retirees downsizing from LA and Pasadena who want cultural amenities without urban density.

  • Claremont's current median sale price is approximately $1.1M, though prices range widely from $600K in the Village area to over $2.3M in Stone Canyon.   • Claremont homes average 40 days on market, reflecting a deliberate market where buyers take time to find the right fit. Properly priced homes in walkable areas near the colleges and Village move faster. Paul Fernandez averages 25 days across all his listings by pricing precisely from day one.
  • Claremont's 2.4 months of supply indicates a seller's market, though conditions are moderately competitive rather than frenzied. The limited housing stock and consistent demand from college-connected buyers keep inventory tight. Sellers who price to current comps maintain leverage without extended sitting periods.
  • The Claremont Colleges consortium drives a buyer demographic unlike any other IE city — academics, professionals, and culturally motivated buyers who prioritize walkability, tree-lined streets, and community character.
