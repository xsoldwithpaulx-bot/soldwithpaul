Living & Selling in Chino Hills

Chino Hills is San Bernardino County's premium residential market — a master-planned city carved into the rolling hills between Diamond Bar and Corona that consistently delivers some of the highest median prices in the Inland Empire. The Chino Valley Unified School District anchors property values, with Ayala High School drawing families from across the IE who prioritize academic quality and are willing to pay the Chino Hills premium over neighboring Chino, Ontario, or Eastvale.

The city's geography creates natural neighborhood tiers. The hilltop communities along Peyton Drive and in The Vellano offer panoramic views and luxury homes above $1.5M. Mid-range master-planned developments like Woodview and Rolling Ridge serve the $800K-$1.2M move-up segment with newer construction, community pools, and trail access. The neighborhoods closer to Carbon Canyon and the 71 freeway corridor provide entry-level single-family homes in the $700K-$900K range that draw first-time buyers upgrading from Chino and Montclair.

Buyer demand in Chino Hills comes from two primary directions: Orange County professionals who want more house for their money without sacrificing school quality, and IE families moving up from Rancho Cucamonga, Ontario, and Fontana. The 71 and 60 freeways provide OC commute access, while the 15 connects to the broader IE. That dual-county positioning gives Chino Hills sellers access to a buyer pool that spans two of California's largest metro economies.
