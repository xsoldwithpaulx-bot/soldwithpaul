Frequently Asked Questions — Choosing a Realtor in La Habra

What is the median home price in La Habra, CA?
According to March 2026 Infosparks and Realtor.com data, the median listing price in La Habra is approximately $914,000 — up nearly 4% month-over-month, one of the stronger appreciation rates in North Orange County. La Habra's price-per-square-foot sits near $1,628, reflecting the city's mix of mid-century ranch homes in central La Habra and newer construction in the 90631 hillside corridor near Country Club Estates. The market has a wider price spread than Buena Park or Cerritos — entry-level La Habra condos transact near $550,000 while larger single-family hillside homes routinely close above $1.5 million. Sellers should benchmark against specific ZIP and property type rather than citywide averages.
How long does it take to sell a house in La Habra?
The median days on market in La Habra is currently 45 days based on March 2026 listing data — longer than Buena Park (29 days) or Cerritos but faster than much of Los Angeles County. That figure has declined roughly 4% from the prior month, indicating absorption is keeping pace with new inventory. Properly priced La Habra homes with professional photography and pre-listing preparation routinely close in under 30 days, particularly in the 90631 ZIP. Homes priced above comparable sales or lacking visual presentation tend to sit 60-75 days. The agents in this ranking typically list their La Habra properties 10-15 days below the citywide median.
Is La Habra a buyer's or seller's market right now?
La Habra is a moderate seller's market as of early 2026. Active listings are at 66 — a manageable supply level — but listing prices are rising month-over-month, median days on market is declining, and no recent listings had price reductions. New listing volume in March 2026 is actually down slightly from February, meaning fresh inventory isn't flooding the market and pressuring sellers. The dynamic is more balanced than fast-moving Buena Park but clearly favors prepared sellers. Properly priced, professionally marketed La Habra homes in the 90631 ZIP are seeing multiple offers within two weekends of listing.
What are the best neighborhoods in La Habra to sell a home?
La Habra is organized around three ZIP codes that each have distinct seller dynamics. The 90631 ZIP (central and eastern La Habra, including Country Club Estates, Westridge, and hillside neighborhoods) sees the strongest transaction volume and commands the highest price points. The 90632 ZIP (P.O. box only, limited residential inventory) sees less consistent activity. The 90633 ZIP (P.O. box only, limited residential inventory) is similarly thin. For most sellers, 90631 is where La Habra's residential market actually operates — and within 90631, the hillside corridors near La Habra Heights command premium pricing while the flats closer to Whittier Boulevard represent the entry-level segment. Pricing comparisons should always be specific to the exact neighborhood and property type.
How do I choose the right real estate agent in La Habra?
Match the agent's La Habra-specific transaction history to your property type and ZIP code. An agent with 30+ La Habra sales in the last three years (like Ray Fernandez above) is categorically more useful than an agent with 200 career sales but only 1 in La Habra — local market knowledge compounds at the ZIP-code level. Ask every agent you interview for three specific data points: their median days on market in La Habra specifically (not citywide or OC-wide), their list-to-sold ratio over the last 24 months, and the names of two recent La Habra sellers you can contact as references. Any agent's California license status can be verified through the DRE public license lookup, and legitimate La Habra agents will volunteer their license number without being asked.
