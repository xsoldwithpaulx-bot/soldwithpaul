Typical seller costs in Southern California include agent commissions, escrow and title fees, transfer taxes, and any agreed-upon repairs or credits. In Los Angeles County, the transfer tax is $1.10 per $1,000 of the sale price, plus city-specific taxes may apply. Total closing costs for sellers generally range from 6% to 8% of the sale price. Paul provides a detailed seller net sheet during your consultation so you know exactly what to expect before listing.

Market conditions vary, but Paul's listings average approximately 25 days on market, which is well below the regional average. The timeline depends on pricing strategy, property condition, and current buyer demand. Homes that are properly staged, professionally photographed, and strategically priced typically sell fastest. Paul's approach focuses on generating maximum interest in the first two weeks to create competitive offers.

A list-to-sold ratio above 100% means the agent consistently sells homes at or above the original asking price. The average agent in Southern California typically achieves 97%–99%. Paul's career list-to-sold ratio is 103%, meaning his sellers net more than their asking price on average. This is achieved through strategic pricing, professional marketing, and skilled negotiation to create competitive bidding situations.

Preparation depends on your property's condition and price point, but general steps include: decluttering and depersonalizing living spaces, addressing minor repairs (leaky faucets, chipped paint, broken fixtures), deep cleaning carpets and windows, improving curb appeal with fresh landscaping, and considering professional staging. Paul provides a customized pre-listing checklist during your initial consultation and can connect you with trusted contractors, stagers, and cleaning services.

Yes, you can sell your home as-is in California. However, California law still requires sellers to complete disclosure forms about known material defects. Selling as-is typically means you will not make repairs requested by the buyer, but it may result in a lower sale price. Paul has experience with estate sales, probate properties, and as-is transactions and can advise you on whether repairs or selling as-is will net you more money.

Spring (March through May) is traditionally the strongest selling season in Southern California due to higher buyer demand, longer daylight hours for showings, and families wanting to move before the school year. However, Southern California's mild climate means the market stays active year-round. Low inventory periods in fall and winter can also work in a seller's favor due to less competition.
